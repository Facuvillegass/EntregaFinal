My CV as a Story

As I always liked writing, my CV will be my own story!

Once upon a time there was a boy who, for as long as he can remember, has been a soccer fanatic. His loved ones quickly realized that his dream of being a professional player would not come true, so they began to think of a different future, although not far from the ball.

They taught him to read when he was three years old and his first practices were with the sports section of the newspaper. Little Facu became a fan of his readings and wanted to take a step forward, when he began to watch videos and memorize match stories, and then recreate them in his home's yard. This custom lasted over time and caused that boy, who was now 17 years old, to decide to study sports journalism.

Entering adulthood, Facu quickly got a job at a soccer radio station. Although he never received financial remuneration, he had enormous professional possibilities, from narrating in a full stadium, to covering a World Cup. This caused his dream to spread to the famous city of Buenos Aires, where journalism in general enjoys a certain wealth that is not even remotely seen in the interior of the country.

In his first attempt to stabilize in the city of fury, Facu spent five months knocking on doors and trying to generate a network of contacts that could open up a job opportunity. Unfortunately, the result was not what was expected and our protagonist had to return to his hometown.

Two years later, Facu would return to the country's capital to try again, with the difference that, from Cordoba, he had already signed a contract with a television channel.

Everything was different in the second stage in Capital Federal. Job opportunities appeared quickly and the second try really seemed like the final one. However, the newspaper industry was going through a bad economic time, which led Facu, after completing a year of uninterrupted work, to say goodbye to his dreams...
